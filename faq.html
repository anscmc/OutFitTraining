Mobile App Questions


Login

Where can I find the app to download?

To download the app, you can click on the link found on the OutFit Training website or you can search ‘OutFit Training’ in the Apple Store or Google Play Store and download.


How do I create an account?

To create an account, you must open the OutFit Training App - login using your email and create a custom password - under the “Get Started” feature upon opening the app. You may also use your Facebook, Google Account, or Apple ID to quickly create an account - Just know that your OutFit login password will be the same for whichever social platform you use (Facebook, Google, Apple)


I never got my verification code; how do I get one?

Be sure to check your spam email for your verification code, sometimes based on your settings, it will be sent there.


How do I reset my password?

To reset your password, simply select “Forgot password” on the login screen. You should then receive an email link to reset your password. Be sure to check your spam if you aren’t seeing the password reset email in your inbox. If you aren’t receiving the password reset email at all, be sure to see the question below.


Why am I not receiving a password reset email?

If you logged in using a SSO (single sign-on option) like your Facebook, Google Account, or Apple ID then your password for OutFit will be the same as that platform. If you used Facebook, Google, or Apple and still want to change your password you must change it on the respective interface, for it to be changed in OutFit App.


Booking

How do I book a class?

To book a class you must download the OutFit Training App from the Apple Store or Google Play Store, and create a profile via your email, Facebook, Google account, or Apple ID. Once your account is created and you are in the app, you will see class options based on your area. Simply select the class time that works best for you and click “Book Now”. If you don’t see any times that work from the short list, select “see all” which is to the right of “Suggested workouts this week” and that will show you all the class time options to book.


How do I update my billing or payment information?

To update payments, select the “Profile” tab, within the OutFit Training App, and click the 3 dots icon in the top right corner. Next you must select “Payment” from the list. There you can choose to add a new credit card or delete an existing payment method. One form of payment must always be entered into your profile for you to book classes.

If you are having trouble switching your method of payment that is on file in the OutFit Training App, please Contact Us and we will help you fix it as soon as possible.


How do I update my personal information?

To update personal information simply click the “Profile” tab in the bottom right corner of your screen in the OutFit Training App. Next you must click “Edit Profile” and update your information.


How do I view my upcoming training sessions?

To view you upcoming workouts, select the “Workout” tab in the OutFit Training App. In that tab you can view your upcoming and completed workouts as well as your friend’s progress.


How do I see which OICs are teaching workouts?

To see which trainers are teaching workouts you should select the “Home” tab on the bottom left of the app and select the Outpost or Mobile van in your area. There you will find specific information on locations, classes, and OICs.


Settings

Should I allow the app to access my location?

Yes. You should allow the app to access your location while using the app so you can easily find workouts in your area. If you do not feel comfortable allowing the app to access your location, you can manually input your address or zip code where you wish to train.


Should I enable push notifications?

Yes. You should enable push notifications so you will be notified when you have an upcoming class, information regarding classes you have signed up for, and can also receive information on special limited time offers.

Introduction To OutFit

What is OutFit?

OutFit is an innovative mobile fitness service that brings convenient, affordable, expertly led private training and group fitness classes to parks and neighborhoods across America. Our franchised fleet of world class gyms-on-wheels are owned and operated by local TRX-certified trainers and are networked via a cutting-edge technology platform and member app. OutFit takes our workouts outside—where we feel our best! Energized by sunshine, blue skies, fresh air — and by an entire community of new friends!


What is the OutFit story?

It began almost a decade ago as Founder, Randy Hetrick's, Mobile marketing initiative at TRX® Dubbed “TRX on Tour,” and manned by TRX Master Trainers, these tricked-out mobile sprinter vans, delivered amazing “flash mob” style group workouts all across the country for over two years. However, delivering such experiences on a grand scale was a pipe-dream in the technological landscape of the time. But Randy dreamed that, one day, the tech might catch up to the concept.

Fast-forward ten years. Technology had evolved to power the likes of MindBody, Classpass, and Uber. The time had finally arrived. Randy’s dream of delivering TRX’s incredible training experiences into neighborhoods across America was finally a realistic possibility.

Then along came COVID-19. Instantly, many of the fitness industry’s most seasoned innovators found themselves out of work — casualties of the COVID shutdown and the layoffs that followed. In what might otherwise have taken years, OutFit’s “Dream Team” — with more than a century of Industry experience between them — coalesced to launch the most path-breaking fitness venture in decades.

Built and tested in the cold of the North
In the cold north of Pennsylvania stands one of the country’s great unsung manufacturing cities, Erie, PA — the place where key members of OutFit’s sales and operations team call home. Erie is where its Vans and Outposts are built, tested, and made ready for service in streets across the country. It is also OutFit’s R&D lab, where equipment and workout protocols are tested against the bitter cold of winter and the stifling heat of summer. The very first OutFit workouts were delivered to our excited first customers, “OutFitters” one and all, in chilly temperatures and three feet of snow on the ground.

Launched with pride in a city full with belief
The City of Plantation, Florida became OutFit’s first stop outside Erie, PA. The match couldn’t have been more perfect. Entrepreneurship, health, fitness, and service to the community—the cornerstones of OutFit’s culture—were equally shared by Mayor Lynn Stoner, the City Council, and the people of Plantation.

Plantation’s people were as warm as the Florida sunshine. They embraced OutFit—with its charter to train the city’s 1st Responders and their families free of charge — from the day we opened. And the business took off.

Close on the heels of Plantation, the City of Fort Lauderdale welcomed OutFit with open arms — as did Wilton Manners, Sunrise, Davie, and others — each queuing up to bring the benefits of OutFit to their citizens and 1st Responders.

With the generous support of those progressive, entrepreneurial cities in South Florida, OutFit is on a roll.


What is an OIC and AOIC?

An OIC is an Outfitter-In-Charge. They are trainers who are Certified Personal Trainers as well as TRX certified - Both certifications are requirements for becoming an OIC. They will be the ones teaching all the workouts and are responsible for providing you with a great experience.

An AOIC is an Assistant Outfitter-In-Charge. AOIC’s have the same certifications as the OIC’s but with a few less duties/responsibilities - think of them as part-time OIC’s.

Membership

Who can be on my Family Plan?


Primary Member: The designated Primary Member is typically the person purchasing the subscription membership. The Primary Member may cancel the entire Family Plan subscription, change the monthly payment method, add or subtract additional members, authorize account purchases for family members, and update subscription membership information.

Additional Member(s):
All other members, other than the Primary Member, are considered Additional Members. The classification of an Additional Member MAY require proof of cohabitation, familial guardianship, age of Additional Member, or residency. Family Plan Memberships are only permitted to have 1 “Additional Member” over the age of 26.

  • Partner Member: The partner member is a single person added to the Primary Membership that is over the age of 26 who executes a General Terms Agreement, Member Services Agreement or other documents agreeing to be jointly and severally liable for all obligations associated with the Primary Membership.
  • Child Member(s): The Child Member(s) is a person(s) added to the Primary Membership that is over the age of 18 and under the age of 26 who executes a General Terms Agreement, Member Services Agreement.
  • Minor Member(s): The Primary Member’s child or children that the Primary Member is the legal guardian of, in such that the Primary Member has the right and authority to make decisions concerning the care, custody, and control of, including entering a Membership on their behalf are considered Minor Members. Minor Members are the age of 3 months, but under the age of 18.
  • Caregiver: A Caregiver, who is over the age of 18, may be added to a Membership, as long as the Membership has at least one Minor Member. A Caregiver could be someone like a family Nanny or Au Pair.
  • Disabled Members: We welcome disabled members. If a disabled member must be accompanied by a non-member assistant or caregiver to reasonably accommodate the member’s use of the service, they can be added.

    • *Any abuse of the above is subject to membership subscription cancellation*

    Workouts

    What are the workouts like?

    OutFit’s workouts are coach-led, group training, and are tailored to you via our unique timer system, so that you can be challenged and pushed specific to your level of training. OutFit’s color-coded timing system is simple to understand and is self-gratifying when effectively applied. It individualizes and gamifies each challenge you take on throughout the workout.


    I haven’t worked out in a while, are the workouts hard?

    The workouts are designed for you, to get out what you put into them. They are tailored to fit any stage of one’s fitness journey. OutFit classes are HIIT (high intensity interval training) style workouts with colored timers (green, yellow, and red) signaling the option to start, push yourself a little further (if you can, rest, and transition to the next exercises.


    How long are the workouts?

    All OutFit workouts are 60 minutes with built in rest periods between rounds and exercises. This 60 minutes includes 5-10 minutes of warm-ups/cool downs.


    How old do you have to be to take class?

    Anyone under the age of 13 must be accompanied by an adult.


    What should I bring to the workout?

    We recommend that you wear a workout tracking device (i.e., Apple watch, Garmin watch, MyZone, Fitbit, etc.) if you have one. We also recommend you bring a full water bottle and towel.


    What should I wear?

    Show up in any workout gear that you would usually wear based on the current climate in your area. Be sure to also wear training shoes, running shoes, tennis shoes, etc.


    How early should I get there for before my first class?

    We encourage you to arrive 15 minutes early to your first class so that you can meet your OIC and be briefed on what to expect from the workout.


    Will someone show me how it works before the workout starts?

    If you arrive early enough (15 minutes early is recommended), your OIC should be able to give you an explanation of how OutFit works. If you don’t arrive early enough, don’t worry, the OIC will still brief you on what the workout will look like in the Frame-up of the workout.


    Is there running in the workouts?

    There is running in some of the workouts but not all of them. The running that is programmed into some of the workouts are brief and short; there is no long-distance running.


    Is there a lot of cardio?

    There can be if you want there to be! The harder you push yourself the more “cardio” you are going to get out of it. But there is always a more cardio-specific station within each workout.

    What is the weather protocol?

    For thunderstorms, hurricanes, tornadoes, extreme high winds, etc. OICs have a protocol they are meant to follow to determine if they will have to cancel the workout or not. If they DO have to cancel due to any of these inclement weather issues in the area, then you will be notified. If it is simply raining, then class will still go on! Who doesn’t love training in the rain!?

    For immense heat, the trainer will be sure to keep you safe and hydrated throughout the workout.

    For cold weather climates if the temperature drops below 20°F, then class will be canceled. If the conditions seem to be too Icy, the OIC is responsible for making sure it is safe for everyone and will notify you about whether the conditions are safe enough for training.

    Booking a class

    How do I book?

    To book a class, you must click on the “Workouts” tab on the OutFit Training app and select “Find a workout”. Once selected you will be able to type in location via the zip code of where you wish to train. The app will then show you the nearest OutFit Training venues. Next, you must click on the OutFit Training venue you wish to attend a class at, find the class time that works best for you, and select “Book Now”.


    Why can’t I book the class at the time I want?

    Be sure to check which tier of workout you are trying to book. If the workout is a TEAM workout and you only have an OPEN subscription, you will not be able to book in for that workout.

    Also, if the class you are wanting to attend is at max capacity, you will also not be able to book. If this is the case, be sure to check back in the days/hours leading up to the workout, as people might cancel, and you may be able to take that spot that becomes available.


    How do I change my subscription tier?

Once you click on the “OPEN” block, you will see all the options for OPEN pricing and the opportunities to downgrade or upgrade.

    If you would like to upgrade or downgrade at the TEAM or PRIVATE tiers, it must be done by canceling your current subscription and re-subscribing at whatever tier you would like to.

    OIC and Franchisees:


    Becoming an OIC (Outfitter-in-Charge)

    What is an OIC?

    The OutFitter-In-Charge (OIC) is among the organization’s most important roles. The OIC is where the rubber meets the road, literally and figuratively. OIC is responsible for building and maintaining each tier of OutFit’s subscriber base within his/her assigned territory. Responsible for achieving subscription and revenue goals for the assigned territory, and for building lasting relationships with the members living within that territory. A successful OIC not only provides exquisite care for existing subscribers but devotes equal energy to soliciting new business. This individual is self-motivated, accountable, and consistently delivers experiences that deliver results and delight your customers. The ideal OIC displays a fierce Owner/Operator mentality and views the van as his/her personal business, and the subscribers as his/her personal guests. He/she will not settle for good enough. Ever.


    What are the responsibilities of an OIC?

  • Responsible for delivering fun and effective exercise experiences to OutFit’s customers
  • Understand and manage the business to achieve your territory’s goals
  • Build and maintain your territory’s forecasted subscriber base at each tier of service: Open, Team & Private
  • Meet or exceed monthly/quarterly/annual subscription targets and revenue goals
  • Coach clients with safe, fun, appropriate exercises, selecting progressions that they can successfully perform to standard. Be a great coach that inspires your clients
  • Consult clients on safety concerns and exercise goals. Expertly demonstrate all required exercise movements and maintain the highest standards of professionalism & integrity
  • Effectively manage class sessions and communicate well with all members
  • Maintain a high level of personal fitness to effectively lead by example
  • Support Open Class requirements within your territory and participate in appropriate local marketing & promotional activities to build subscribership
  • Skillfully support the application and achievement of permits for your training venues.
  • Maintain van and all training equipment at highest level of care, attention, & sanitation
